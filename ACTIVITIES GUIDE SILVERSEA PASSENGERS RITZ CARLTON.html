Santiago rewards the curious traveller. From South America's highest observation deck to legendary underground wine cellars, from a century-old hillside funicular to world-class pre-Columbian art — the city offers a genuinely varied menu of experiences, most of which require no advance reservation and are easy to navigate independently. We always recommend using Uber or a trusted taxi for transfers, and to remain attentive to your personal belongings in all public spaces.

⚠️
Personal Safety — Always Keep in Mind

Always use Uber or InDriver rather than hailing unmarked taxis. Keep your mobile phone in a pocket — not in hand — while walking in busy areas. Keep cameras, bags, and wallets secure. The areas in this guide are among Santiago's safest, but attentiveness is always wise. If in doubt, ask your hotel concierge.

Zone One
Las Condes & Providencia
📍 Las Condes & Providencia
Easy · No physical effort · Fully accessible
Sky Costanera ObservatoryObservation Deck · City Views
Gran Torre Costanera (Costanera Center Tower), Av. Andrés Bello 2425, Providencia · Floors 61 & 62
From Ritz-Carlton 5–10 min by taxi or metro · Tobalaba Metro Station (Lines 1 & 4) nearby

The highest observation point in South America — at 300 metres above street level, the 360° view from floors 61 and 62 encompasses the full Santiago basin, the snowcapped Andes to the east, and the coastal range to the west. Labelled panoramic windows identify every landmark below. The Sky 300 Bar & Coffee serves cocktails and light fare at altitude — sunset visits are particularly spectacular.

Sky Costanera is located inside Costanera Center, one of the largest and most complete shopping malls in Latin America — with over 300 stores, a full-service food court, restaurants, a cinema complex, and easy covered access. Tickets can be purchased at the ground floor box office or online in advance (recommended on weekends to skip queues). High-speed glass elevator; fully accessible for all mobility levels.

Hours Mon–Sun 10:00–22:00 (last lift 21:00)
Entry ~CLP 18,000 adults (~USD 18)
Tip Check sky clarity · Best at sunset

Parque AraucanoUrban Park · Leisure · Seasonal Events
Pdte. Riesco 5877, Las Condes · Open daily 06:00–21:00 · Free entry
From Ritz-Carlton 10–20 min by Uber

A 30-hectare urban park just minutes from the hotel — Las Condes' green lung and one of Santiago's most pleasant outdoor spaces. Mature woodland paths are ideal for an early morning or late afternoon walk, with views of the Andes on clear days. The park features well-maintained jogging and cycling trails, picnic areas under shade trees, sports courts, a skate park, and the popular "Aguas Danzantes" — dancing water fountains at ground level, a natural draw on warm days. Parque Arauco mall is adjacent to the park, offering restaurants and shopping immediately after your walk.

The park also hosts seasonal events: an outdoor cinema festival in January, a winter ice rink (June–August), the Festival de la Rosa (spring), and regular live music organised by the municipality.

Hours Daily 06:00–21:00
Entry Free
Seasonal Ice rink (winter) · Outdoor cinema (Jan)

MUT — Mercado Urbano TobalabaUrban Market · Gastronomy · Design
Av. Tobalaba 1838, Providencia · 1 metro stop from the Ritz-Carlton, or 5 min walk

MUT is one of Santiago's most vibrant urban market concepts — a curated space where gastronomy, local design, sustainability, and community converge. Located in Providencia near Metro Tobalaba, the market brings together local food producers, artisan vendors, independent coffee roasters, and emerging designers under one creative roof. An excellent spot to sample Chilean street food, browse handmade crafts, and get a feel for the city's contemporary creative scene. The format mixes permanent stalls with rotating weekend events and pop-up fairs, making each visit slightly different. A great complement to a visit to Sky Costanera, which is just minutes away.

From Ritz-Carlton 1 metro stop (Tobalaba Station) · or walkable from the hotel

Zone Two
Cerro San Cristóbal — Parque Metropolitano
📍 Bellavista / Providencia 🚗 10–20 min by Uber from Ritz-Carlton

Santiago's great urban park — over 700 hectares of gardens, trails, and botanical spaces. The hill has two main access points: Pedro de Valdivia Norte / Oasis Station (east side, ideal for the cable car), and Pío Nono / Bellavista (west side, for the funicular and the zoo). Both connect at the summit. A second map below this section shows the park's internal layout and all attractions by position on the hill.

Easy · Fully accessible by cable car or funicular
Teleférico (Cable Car)Aerial Cable Car · Recommended Entry · Pedro de Valdivia Norte
Oasis Station — Av. El Cerro 750, Pedro de Valdivia Norte · Tue–Sun 10:00–19:45

The most scenic and recommended way to ascend Cerro San Cristóbal. From Oasis Station, the cable car climbs through park vegetation in three connected stops: Oasis, Tupahue, and Cumbre (summit) — a 10-minute aerial journey with uninterrupted panoramic views of the city and Andes. At the summit, you can continue by funicular down the other side to Pío Nono — an excellent full-crossing experience. A second Pío Nono cable car line (opened 2025) adds flexibility near the zoo entrance.

Access Uber to "Oasis Station, Pedro de Valdivia Norte"
Hours Tue–Sun 10:00–19:45 · Closed Mon AM
Entry All-inclusive park pass "Vive el Parque" ~CLP 8,450 · cable car + funicular
Accessibility Wheelchair accessible

Funicular — Pío Nono entrance (Bellavista / west side)
Inaugurated in 1925, declared a National Historical Monument in 2000. Each car holds 40 passengers and climbs from Plaza Caupolicán (Pío Nono 445, Bellavista) to the summit in 5–7 minutes. Access from Bellavista — searchable on Uber as "Funicular San Cristóbal Pío Nono." Same combo ticket as the cable car.

At the hill's highest point stands the 22-metre statue of the Virgin Mary, visible from across the entire city. The surrounding sanctuary and esplanade offer the best 360° view in central Santiago — the Andes, the city grid, and Costanera Center far below. Free to visit once at the summit.

Zone Three
Historic Old Town & Cultural Centre
📍 Downtown Santiago 🚗 15–25 min by Uber from Ritz-Carlton

Santiago's historic heart is compact, walkable between landmarks, and home to three of the city's most remarkable museums — all within a few blocks of Plaza de Armas. A half-day here covers the key sites at a comfortable pace. The downtown area is busy; keep personal belongings secure.

Easy · Flat ground · Fully accessible
Museo Chileno de Arte PrecolombinoMuseum · Pre-Columbian Art · World-Class
Bandera 361, Downtown Santiago (1 block from Plaza de Armas)

Widely regarded as one of the finest museums in Latin America, housed in the beautifully restored Palacio de la Real Aduana (1807). The collection spans over 10,000 years of pre-Hispanic civilisation — Maya, Aztec, Inca, Mapuche, Chinchorro, and Amazonian cultures. All labels in English and Spanish. Allow 2–3 hours. The museum shop carries excellent art books and quality artisan pieces.

Hours Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00 (last entry 17:15) · Closed Mon
Languages All labels in English & Spanish
Duration 2–3 hours
Website ↗
Museo Nacional de Bellas ArtesMuseum · Chilean Fine Art · Free Entry
José Miguel de la Barra 650, Parque Forestal

South America's oldest fine art museum (1880), housed in the stunning neoclassical Palacio de Bellas Artes — a glass-domed Beaux-Arts landmark modelled on the Petit Palais in Paris. Over 300 works span Chilean and Latin American art from the colonial period to the present. Entry completely free for all visitors. Located in pleasant tree-lined Parque Forestal along the Mapocho River.

Hours Tue–Sun 10:00–18:30 · Closed Mon
Entry Free for all visitors
Duration 1.5–2 hours
Website ↗
Plaza de Armas & Palacio de La MonedaHistoric Centre · Architecture · Changing of the Guard
Plaza de Armas / Morandé 80, Downtown Santiago

Santiago's colonial heart, founded in 1541 — the grand Metropolitan Cathedral, National History Museum, and Central Post Office all face the square. Ten minutes south: La Moneda, Chile's neoclassical presidential palace (1805), where the changing of the guard takes place every other day at 10:00 AM in the central courtyard — open to the public and free. The palace courtyards and Plaza de la Ciudadanía are accessible at no charge. Keep belongings secure in busy Plaza de Armas.

Cerro Santa LucíaUrban Hill · Gardens · City Viewpoint
Santa Lucía Street, Downtown Santiago

A 230-foot natural hill in the middle of downtown — the site where Pedro de Valdivia proclaimed the founding of Santiago in 1541. Terraced stone staircases wind up through ornamental gardens, fountains, and neo-Gothic terraces to several viewpoints offering an intimate city-level view of Santiago's rooftops and the Andes. The ascent takes 10–15 minutes. Particularly lovely at golden hour. Fully free; elevator available for those who prefer not to climb.

Hours Open daily, dawn to dusk
Entry Free
Duration 45–60 min at leisure
Best time Late afternoon · Golden hour

Zone Four
Bellavista & Lastarria neighbourhood
📍 Bellavista / Providencia / Lastarria 🚗 20–30 min by Uber depending on time of day and traffic

Two of Santiago's most characterful neighbourhoods, both on foot from each other and from the base of Cerro San Cristóbal. Bellavista is known for its street art, the Neruda house, and its buzzing restaurant scene. Lastarria is its more architectural and cultural counterpart — quieter, more bohemian, and packed with galleries, coffee shops, and weekend markets.

Easy · Flat neighbourhood walks
La Chascona — Casa Museo Pablo NerudaHouse Museum · Poetry · Cultural Heritage
Fernando Márquez de la Plata 0192, Bellavista

Pablo Neruda built this whimsical labyrinthine house in 1953 as a private sanctuary for his great love, Matilde Urrutia — "La Chascona" (the wild-haired one). After Neruda's death in 1973, Matilde restored the home and opened it to the public. The split-level house is packed with the poet's collections: African masks, a miniature bar shaped like a ship's cabin, and portraits of Matilde by Diego Rivera. Audio guides available in English. Approximately 1–1.5 hours.

Hours Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00 (to 19:00 in summer) · Closed Mon
Duration 1–1.5 hours
Website ↗
Bellavista Neighbourhood WalkStreet Art · Boutiques · Coffee Culture · San Cristóbal Access
Centred on Pío Nono, Constitución, and Loreto streets, Providencia

A self-guided wander through Santiago's most visually vibrant neighbourhood — large-scale murals cover entire building facades, design stores sit alongside artisan leather workshops, and small cafés pull excellent single-origin Chilean coffee. The neighbourhood's dual personality — quiet and leafy by day, buzzing with restaurants and bars by evening — makes it worth visiting at any hour.

Note: at the far end of Bellavista Street (towards the Mapocho River), one of the two main entrances to Cerro San Cristóbal is accessible — the Pío Nono funicular base. The walk from the Neruda house to this entrance takes about 5 minutes, making it a natural pairing with a cable car or funicular ascent. Keep your phone in a pocket when admiring street art.

Duration 1–2 hrs at leisure
Entry Free

Lastarria neighbourhoodCultural Quarter · Heritage Architecture · Weekend Markets
José Victorino Lastarria street & surroundings, Providencia (near Cerro Santa Lucía)

One of Santiago's most emblematic and bohemian neighbourhoods. Located close to Cerro Santa Lucía, it blends history, art, gastronomy, and urban life in an atmosphere full of coffee shops, bookshops, galleries, and heritage architecture. Born in the mid-19th century with the construction of the Iglesia de la Veracruz, it has since become one of the city's key cultural poles and is today classified as a "Zona Típica" for its historical and architectural value.

On weekends, Lastarria comes alive with markets selling handmade crafts, second-hand books, illustrations, and independent design along José Victorino Lastarria street and around Plaza Mulato Gil. Street musicians, artists, and a distinctly cosmopolitan energy give the neighbourhood its unique character.

From Ritz-Carlton ~20–30 min by Uber
Best time Weekend mornings for markets

Museo de Artes Visuales (MAVI)
A contemporary visual arts museum housed in a striking building in the Lastarria neighbourhood. The collection focuses on Chilean artists from the late 20th century to the present, with rotating exhibitions of painting, sculpture, photography, and installation art. One of the best spaces in Santiago to see current Chilean visual arts. Closed Mondays.

Centro Cultural Gabriela Mistral (GAM)
One of Chile's most active cultural centres, built on the site of the former UNCTAD III building (1972) and reopened in 2010 as a tribute to Nobel Prize-winning poet Gabriela Mistral. Hosts theatre, dance, live music, exhibitions, and creative fairs year-round. The building's architecture — an open, publicly accessible complex — is itself worth seeing. Entry to most common areas and many events is free.

Iglesia de la Veracruz
One of the oldest churches in the neighbourhood and a symbol of Lastarria's origin. This 19th-century neo-Renaissance church is a declared Historical Monument of Chile. Its modest but beautiful interior and heritage façade make it a worthwhile stop on a walking tour of the barrio.

🌳
Plaza Mulato Gil de Castro
A charming cobblestoned square at the heart of Lastarria, surrounded by heritage buildings housing galleries, antique shops, and outdoor café terraces. On weekends it fills with artisan market stalls selling ceramics, illustrations, vintage clothing, and design objects. One of Santiago's most photographed urban spaces, with a relaxed, creative energy that invites lingering.

Templo Bahá'í de Sudamérica
📍 Diagonal Las Torres 2000, Peñalolén 🚗 30–45 min by Uber — time may vary significantly depending on hour and day
Moderate · 155 steps to the temple · Ramps available
Templo Bahá'í — Temple & GardensArchitecture · Spirituality · Panoramic Views
Diagonal Las Torres 2000, Peñalolén

One of only nine Bahá'í temples in the world — and the only one in South America. Inaugurated in 2016, this extraordinary architectural landmark sits at over 1,000 metres above sea level on the Andean foothills, with a sweeping panoramic view of the entire Santiago valley. Designed to resemble a flower in bloom, the temple's nine translucent marble and glass "petals" create a luminous, cathedral-like interior of extraordinary beauty and calm. The surrounding gardens feature native Andean flora and nine reflecting pools. The temple welcomes visitors of all backgrounds for meditation and reflection; no religious affiliation or advance booking required. Access the temple via Uber directly to "Templo Bahái Santiago" — the winding access road is not suitable for walking. Respectful attire appreciated inside the temple.

Viña Concha y ToroWinery · Wine Tasting · Chilean Heritage
Virginia Subercaseaux 210, Pirque · Tours daily 09:00–17:00

Chile's most famous winery, founded in 1883. The recently redesigned Wine Center offers a fully guided experience: the Casillero del Diablo Sensory Experience through the iconic underground cellars, the Guarda del Alto aging cellar, a panoramic Mirador overlooking the Pirque vineyard, and the Varietal Garden. The experience concludes with a tasting of four premium wines. The Plaza Concha y Toro offers dining and an exclusive wine shop. Tours run in English, Spanish, and Portuguese — specify your language when purchasing. Access by Uber to "Viña Concha y Toro, Pirque" or arrange a private transfer via your hotel concierge.

Viña Cousiño MaculUrban Winery · Heritage · Founded 1856
Av. Quilín 7100, Peñalolén

The only surviving 19th-century winery in Chile still in the hands of its founding family. Its historic cellars dating to 1856, colonial hacienda, and vineyards sit approximately 30–45 minutes from The Ritz-Carlton, depending on time of day and traffic. Tours cover the underground cellars, the family gardens, and a tasting of the estate's classic varietals. A more intimate, less commercial experience than Concha y Toro, and ideal for those short on time. Tours in English available.

✦
Practical notes for independent travel

Getting around: Always use Uber or InDriver — safe, reliable, and easy throughout Santiago. Your hotel concierge can also arrange a trusted private driver for the day (~USD 60–90 for a full day), the most comfortable option for combining multiple sites.

Personal safety: Keep your mobile phone in a pocket — not in your hand — while walking in public areas. Keep cameras, bags, and wallets secure. The areas in this guide are among Santiago's safest, but attentiveness is always wise.

Weather & timing: The Andes are clearest in the morning. Check sky conditions before committing to observation visits. Winter (June–August) often brings the clearest mountain visibility of the year.
